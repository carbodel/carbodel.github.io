    Melting glaciers

    Over the past 20 years, the phenomenon of melting glaciers has accelerated on a global scale. 267 billion tons of ice per year are lost every year, with a 130% surge between 2000 and 2019

    Rising sea levels

    Between 1901 and 2020, due to the global warming, a sea level rise of 20 cm was observed, with an average growth of 1.35 mm/year from 1901 to 1990 and an average growth of 3.7 mm/year between 2006 and 2018. Among the evidence of this phenomenon, which threatens the coasts and most of the islands, we can include the increasingly destructive storm surges

    Oceans acidification

    The increase of carbon dioxide in the atmosphere is also affecting the balance of marine ecosystems. The oceans, in fact, absorb between a third and a quarter of all CO2 released into the atmosphere each year. At the expense, for example, are the coral reefs whose survival is already jeopardized by the phenomenon of bleaching that certifies their death

    Wildfires increase

    Wildfires in forests and woods, in the current context of climate change, are a problem destined to worsen in the coming years. We are talking about a phenomenon of enormous size which was the responsible of the destruction of 8.5 million hectars in Europe alone between 2000 and 2017

    Desertification

    The duration of the drought is linked to changes in average rainfall which in turn changes with global warming. This is why regions that are experiencing a decline in average rainfall (such as the Mediterranean, Central and South America and South Africa) experience and will continue to experience more lasting and frequent droughts

    FAQ – Frequently asked questions

    What is a carbon reduction certificate?

    A carbon reduction certificate is a document that certifies the reduction of a certain amount of carbon emissions, usually expressed in tons of CO2 equivalents, resulting from the implementation of measures or projects aimed at mitigating climate change. These certificates are issued by entities or organizations and can be used as evidence of an organization's commitment to reducing its carbon emissions

    What is a carbon measurement certificate?

    A carbon measurement certificate attests that an organization has conducted an accurate and comprehensive assessment of its greenhouse gas emissions, usually in accordance with internationally recognized standards such as the Greenhouse Gas Protocol. This certificate provides a reliable basis for understanding and monitoring the organization's carbon emissions over time, enabling the identification of areas for intervention to reduce environmental impact

    What is a carbon neutrality certificate?

    The carbon neutrality certificate is a document that certifies the neutral environmental (in terms of carbon) impact condition of a given activity. In few words, the amount of carbon dioxide related to company activities, after applying reduction, removal and/or compensation techniques and strategies, is zero. The carbon neutrality certificate is obtained following an accurate calculation process followed by a verification process aimed at identifying and quantifying CO2 equivalent emissions and planning how to reduce and offset them in order to balance them in their entirety

    What is target setting?

    "Target setting" is the process of establishing specific and measurable goals that an organization commits to achieving within a certain period of time. These goals can encompass various aspects of business operations, such as reducing carbon emissions, increasing the proportion of renewable energy used, or improving energy efficiency. Once defined, targets are regularly monitored and evaluated to track progress and make any necessary adjustments to ensure the achievement of the set objectives

    What is the GHG Protocol?

    The GHG Protocol, which stands for Greenhouse Gas Protocol, is a global initiative aimed at developing standards and tools for measuring and managing greenhouse gas emissions. It is one of the primary frameworks used by organizations, governments, and other entities to calculate and report their greenhouse gas emissions consistently and reliably. The GHG Protocol provides detailed guidelines on how to measure and report greenhouse gas emissions, enabling effective comparison and monitoring of environmental performance across different entities. It was developed by the World Resources Institute (WRI) and the World Business Council for Sustainable Development (WBCSD)

    What is SBTi?

    SBTi, which stands for Science Based Targets initiative, is a collaborative effort among various organizations including the Carbon Disclosure Project (CDP), the World Resources Institute (WRI), the World Wide Fund for Nature (WWF), and the United Nations Global Compact. The main goal of SBTi is to assist companies in setting greenhouse gas emissions reduction targets in line with climate science to limit global temperature rise to safe levels, as defined by international agreements such as the Paris Agreement. Companies joining the initiative can commit to setting science-based targets to reduce their greenhouse gas emissions and adhere to transparent and rigorous reporting practices to monitor and communicate their progress.

    Is it possible to have environmental consulting without obtaining the carbon neutrality certificate?

    Of course! We offer consultancy services in terms of energy and environmental sustainability aimed at energy efficiency, at the training of personnel in sustainability matters and at the development of measurement and mitigation strategies of sources of greenhouse gas emissions. This is done totally independently from obtaining the certificate carbon neutrality

    How long does the obtained certificate last?

    This process is conducted on an annual basis and therefore the certificate obtained is valid for the entire year considered in the analysis phase

    Is it possible to certify only one product or one process?

    Yes, in order to certify a given product or process, it is initially necessary to define the system boundaries applicable and related to the given product and/or given service, within which the analysis for the balancing of emissions of CO2in the atmosphere will then occur

    How are the emissions for which a company is responsible calculated?

    The emissions for which a company is responsible are calculated on an annual basis in the form of tons of CO2 equivalent (CO2eq) representing the unit of measurement that allows the overall weighting of different greenhouse gas emissions with different climate-altering effects. The calculations of the Carbon Footprint are performed according to the guidelines of the GHG Protocol Corporate Accounting and Reporting Standard and the dedicated regulatory standards following recognized scientific principles and well-established methodologies

    What are carbon credits?

    Carbon credit is a financial unit that represents the removal of one ton of CO2 equivalent from the atmosphere. It represents the emission of greenhouse gases that have been avoided, reduced or sequestered through a project and which can be purchased as a means of offsetting the emissions. Some of the most famous carbon credits are indicated with one of the following abbreviations: VER (Verified Emission Reduction), CER (Certified Emission Reduction) and VCU (Verified Carbon Unit)

    How are emissions for which a company is responsible classified?

    The emission sources that make up the corporate carbon footprint can be grouped into three macro-classes defined by the GHG Protocol:

    • Scope 1:Direct emissions from installations within the boundaries of the organization due to the use of fossil fuels and the emission of any greenhouse gas into the atmosphere
    • Scope 2: EIndirect emissions deriving from the generation of electricity, heat and steam imported and consumed by the organization
    • Scope 3: Indirect emissions not under the control of the organization due to the activity of the company. That is, emission sources that are not under the direct control of the company, but whose emissions are indirectly due to company activity
